## The Eight Mandatory Sections

| # | Section | Category | What makes it strong |
|---|---------|----------|---------------------|
| 1 | Strategic Context | NEW | Outcome-oriented purpose, connected to "data as an advantage," 2+ trends creating urgency |
| 2 | Who We Serve & What They Need | NEW | 2-3 real personas with workflows, ranked pain, 2+ verbatim quotes |
| 3 | Strategic Focus Areas & Key Initiatives | ELEVATE | 2-3 thematic bets (not feature lists), each tied to user pain + expected outcome |
| 4 | Investment, Capacity & Expected Return | NEW | Specific LOE in sprints/hours, phased, quantified return |
| 5 | Roadmap & Phasing | KEEP | Committed vs. directional distinction, sequencing rationale, named dependencies |
| 6 | Success Metrics | ELEVATE | Leading + lagging indicators, baselines, failure signal per bet |
| 7 | Risks, Dependencies & Asks | ELEVATE | Specific risks with mitigation, named dependency owners, explicit leadership asks |
| 8 | OKR Status & Operating Metrics | KEEP | Honest read-out, at-risk items flagged with recovery paths |

## The Three Tests

Every plan must pass all three before advancing to Stage 3:

**1. Story Test** — Can you tell the plan in 3 minutes without slides?
- Opens with tension (problem/opportunity), not an agenda
- Has a clear point of view — a defensible thesis, not a list of priorities
- The ask is explicit and specific

**2. User Test** — Is every initiative traceable to real user pain?
- Every bet links to a named persona and a ranked pain point from Section 2
- The verbatim quote standard: specific, attributed, creates urgency

**3. Investment Test** — Can you defend LOE against expected return?
- LOE is specific (sprints, not "significant effort")
- Return is quantified (hours saved, error reduction, revenue enabled)
- Tradeoffs are named: what are you NOT doing as a result?

## The Vagueness Test

If a sentence could describe any team's work, it fails. Apply this to every sentence:

| Fails | Passes |
|-------|--------|
| "Improve platform reliability" | "Reduce p95 query latency from 8.2s to under 2s for the analytics data mart by Q3" |
| "Dependency on platform team" | "Dependency on Platform Infra to complete the data lake schema migration by Q2 — owner: [Name]" |
| "Significant engineering effort" | "12 engineering sprints: 3 discovery, 6 build, 2 test, 1 rollout hardening" |
| "Improve analyst experience" | "Reduce weekly data refresh cycle from 4 hours to 20 minutes for 15 analysts" |

## The Six Storytelling Requirements

1. **Open with tension, not agenda** — problem or opportunity creates urgency in 60 seconds
2. **Anchor every bet to a human moment** — name the person and the specific moment the work changes
3. **Have a clear point of view** — defensible thesis, not "here are our priorities"
4. **Make the ask explicit and specific** — a decision, a dependency, a resource needed
5. **The deck is a prop, not a script** — can tell the story with the laptop closed
6. **Anticipate hard questions and answer them first** — "Why this over X?", "How do you know?", "What if it fails?", "What do you need?"

## The Dry Run Process

### Stage 1 — Own Team (Safe Space)
- Present to PMs, POs, engineering leads
- Director observes but doesn't lead feedback
- Goal: stress-test plan foundation and story arc

### Stage 2 — Peer Zone (Constructive Challenge)
- Present to another zone's PM team
- Devil's advocate Q&A simulation
- Goal: test story lands for a less familiar audience

### Stage 3 — Leadership Preview (Pre-Flight Check)
- Present to Jason (optionally Bridget/Vijay)
- Final calibration before executive review
- Outcome: green light or clearly scoped refinement round

### Structured Feedback Dimensions (all stages)
1. **Plan Foundation** — user pain grounding, tradeoffs, asks, cross-team impact
2. **Story & Narrative** — tension opening, point of view, slides independence, hard questions
3. **Presence & Delivery** — ownership, position-holding, data confidence, audience connection
4. **Q&A Readiness** — crisp answers to strategic tradeoffs without reopening slides
